# Data Citation Guidelines

This document lists the required or recommended citations for the datasets used in this project. Always check the dataset website for the latest version- and date-specific citation text.

---

## Alliance Treaty Obligations and Provisions (ATOP)

The ATOP data page requests the following citation:[web:29]

> Leeds, Brett Ashley, Jeffrey M. Ritter, Sara McLaughlin Mitchell, and Andrew G. Long. 2002. “Alliance Treaty Obligations and Provisions, 1815–1944.” *International Interactions* 28(3): 237–260.[web:29]

If you use derived ATOP data (e.g., S-scores), also cite the additional articles specified on the ATOP “Additional Data” page.[web:32]

---

V-Dem provides version-specific citations. For example, for v11 the R package CITATION file gives:[web:34]

> Coppedge, Michael, John Gerring, Carl Henrik Knutsen, Staffan I. Lindberg, Jan Teorell, Nazifa Alizada, David Altman, Michael Bernhard, Agnes Cornell, M. Steven Fish, Lisa Gastaldi, Haakon Gjerløw, Adam Glynn, Allen Hicken, Garry Hindle, Nina Ilchenko, Joshua Krusell, Anna Lührmann, Seraphine F. Maerz, Kyle L. Marquardt, Kelly McMann, Valeriya Mechkova, Juraj Medzihorsky, Pamela Paxton, Daniel Pemstein, Joseﬁne Pernes, Johannes von Römer, Brigitte Seim, Rachel Sigman, Svend-Erik Skaaning, Jeffrey Staton, Aksel Sundström, Eitan Tzelgov, Luca Uberti, Yi-ting Wang, Tore Wig, Steven Wilson, and Daniel Ziblatt. 2021. “V-Dem [Country–Year/Country–Date] Dataset v11.” Varieties of Democracy (V-Dem) Project.[web:34]

For your project, use the exact citation text for the specific V-Dem version you download (e.g., v12, v13) from the V-Dem data page.[web:25][web:37]

## V-Dem Episodes of Regime Transformation / Episodes of Regime Transition

For the Episodes of Regime Transformation (ERT) dataset, V-Dem recommends citing the dataset and associated documentation. A common format is:[web:5]

> Edgell, Amanda B., Seraphine F. Maerz, Laura Maxwell, Kelly M. McMann, Jean Lachapelle, and Staffan I. Lindberg. 2020. *Episodes of Regime Transformation (ERT) Dataset* v2. Varieties of Democracy (V-Dem) Project.[web:5]

Use the version, year, and authors exactly as specified in the ERT codebook and download page.[web:5]

---

## Correlates of War: Militarized Interstate Disputes (MIDs v5)

The COW MIDs data page specifies that you should cite the MID dataset and the methodological article:[web:6]

> Palmer, Glenn, Vito D’Orazio, Michael Kenwick, and Matthew Lane. 2015. “The MID4 Data Set, 2002–2010: Procedures, Coding Rules and Description.” *Conflict Management and Peace Science* 32(2): 222–242.[web:6]

> Correlates of War Project. *Militarized Interstate Disputes (MID) Dataset, Version 5.0*. Correlates of War Project.[web:6]

Include the version number and coverage years in your data appendix.

---

## Correlates of War: National Material Capabilities (NMC)

The NMC dataset page provides this standard citation:[web:7]

> Correlates of War Project. *National Material Capabilities (NMC) Dataset, Version 6.0*. Correlates of War Project.[web:7]

You may also cite the foundational article:

> Singer, J. David. 1988. “Reconstructing the Correlates of War Dataset on Material Capabilities of States, 1816–1985.” *International Interactions* 14(2): 115–132.[web:7]

## Archigos

Use the standard article citation:[web:8]

> Goemans, H. E., Kristian Skrede Gleditsch, and Giacomo Chiozza. 2009. “Introducing Archigos: A Dataset of Political Leaders.” *Journal of Peace Research* 46(2): 269–283.[web:8]

If you rely on a particular Archigos release (e.g., 4.1), indicate the version and download source in a note.

---

## Jeff Colgan’s Revolutionary Leaders Dataset

Colgan’s data page links the dataset to his article on revolutionary leaders.[web:9] Cite both the article and the dataset:

> Colgan, Jeff D. 2013. “Domestic Revolutionary Leaders and International Conflict.” *World Politics* 65(4): 656–696.[web:9]

> Colgan, Jeff D. *Revolutionary Leaders and Revolutions Dataset*, Version 1.1.[web:9]

Mention the version number in your methods or data section.

---

## Jonathan Hanson’s State Capacity Dataset

Hanson and Sigman’s article introduces the state capacity index:[web:10]

> Hanson, Jonathan K., and Rachel Sigman. 2021. “Leviathan’s Latent Dimensions: Measuring State Capacity for Comparative Political Research.” *Journal of Politics* 83(4): 1495–1510.[web:10]

Reference the dataset as the “Hanson and Sigman State Capacity Index” and include any version/date information from your download source (e.g., Our World in Data or the authors’ repository).[web:10]

---

## Fraser Institute Economic Freedom Dataset

The Economic Freedom of the World report provides a recommended citation.[web:11] For the 2022 release:

> Gwartney, James, Robert Lawson, Joshua Hall, and Ryan Murphy. 2022. *Economic Freedom of the World: 2022 Annual Report*. Fraser Institute.[web:11]

When citing the underlying data specifically, you can use:

> Gwartney, James, Robert Lawson, Joshua Hall, and Ryan Murphy. *Economic Freedom of the World Dataset*. Fraser Institute. (Specify the year/version used, e.g., 2022).[web:11]

---

## Maddison Historical Statistics / Maddison Project

The Maddison Project recommends:[web:12]

> Bolt, Jutta, and Jan Luiten van Zanden. 2014. “The Maddison Project: Collaborative Research on Historical National Accounts.” *Economic History Review* 67(3): 627–651.[web:12]

Also mention the Maddison Project Database with its specific version (e.g., “Maddison Project Database 2020”) in your data note.[web:12]

---

## RED – Relational Export Dataset

The RED introducing paper is published via the University of Bremen repository:[web:13]

> Dür, Andreas, Leonardo Baccini, and colleagues. “Introducing RED – The Relational Export Dataset.” (Working/technical paper, University of Bremen).[web:13]

Use the full author list, year, and outlet exactly as they appear in the RED paper, and state the RED dataset version in your appendix.[web:13]

---

## Ross Oil and Gas Dataset

The Harvard Dataverse entry provides an explicit data citation.[web:22] In generic form:

> Ross, Michael L. *Oil and Gas Data, 1932–2014*. Harvard Dataverse. https://doi.org/10.7910/DVN/ZTPW0Y. (Include the Dataverse version number you used.)[web:22]

Follow the exact citation block shown on the Dataverse page when you download the data.[web:22]

## Standardized World Income Inequality Database (SWIID)

The SWIID website states:[web:15]

> Solt, Frederick. 2020. “Measuring Income Inequality Across Countries and Over Time: The Standardized World Income Inequality Database.” *Social Science Quarterly* 101(3): 1183–1199. SWIID Version 9.91, December 2025.[web:15]

Update the version and date to match the specific SWIID release you use (the Dataverse entry lists versions 8–9 and beyond).[web:15][web:16]

---

## Formal Bilateral Influence Capacity (FBIC) Dataset

The FBIC dataset is hosted by the University of Denver’s Pardee Center.[web:24] A suitable citation format is:

> Moyer, Jonathan D., and colleagues. *Formal Bilateral Influence Capacity (FBIC) Index* [Dataset]. Frederick S. Pardee Center for International Futures, Josef Korbel School of International Studies, University of Denver.[web:24]

Include the year, version number, and URL exactly as specified on the FBIC dataset page.[web:24]

---

## Global Leader Ideology Dataset (Herre)

Herre’s dissertation at the University of Chicago describes the Global Leader Ideology Dataset:[web:23]

> Herre, Bastian. 2022. *Global Leader Ideology Dataset*. In “Essays on Political Leaders and Economic Policy” (PhD dissertation, University of Chicago).[web:23]

If the dataset is distributed with a DOI or separate replication package, use the formal citation provided there and note the version you employ.[web:23]

## First Use of Violent Force Dataset (Caprioli & Trumbore)

The dataset is associated with the following article:

> Caprioli, Mary, and Peter F. Trumbore. 2003. “Identifying ‘First Use’ of Violent Force in Militarized Interstate Disputes.” *International Studies Perspectives* 4(2): 153–172.

If there is a standalone replication/data repository, follow its recommended data citation as well, including DOI and version.

---

## Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP)

UCDP requires citation of both the program and each dataset used.[web:20][web:27] A generic pattern is:

> Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP). *UCDP [Name of Dataset] [Version]*. Department of Peace and Conflict Research, Uppsala University. https://ucdp.uu.se.[web:20][web:27]

For example, for the UCDP Battle-Related Deaths Dataset, use the dataset’s own “How to cite” text, including version and year, as specified on the UCDP website.[web:20][web:27]

---

## Dangerous Companions Project Non-State Armed Groups (NAGs) Dataset

The Dangerous Companions / NAGs project website indicates that the data are associated with Belgin San-Akca’s book on external state support for rebel groups.[web:21][web:28] Cite:

> San-Akca, Belgin. 2016. *States in Disguise: Causes of External State Support for Rebel Groups*. New York: Oxford University Press.[web:28]

When referencing the dataset, identify it as the “Non-State Armed Groups (NAGs) Dataset, Dangerous Companions Project (version 2015)” or the specific version indicated on the project website.[web:21][web:28]
